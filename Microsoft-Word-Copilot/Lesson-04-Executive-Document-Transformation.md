# Lesson 4 – Executive Document Transformation

## Objective

Learn how to transform a single executive document into multiple communication products for different audiences while preserving strategic messaging, factual accuracy, and institutional consistency.

## Exercises Completed

### Exercise 1 – President's Campus Message

Transformed an executive briefing into an inspirational campus-wide message suitable for faculty, staff, and students.

### Exercise 2 – Board of Regents Brief

Created a concise executive briefing focused on governance, accountability, institutional risk, strategic priorities, and resource alignment. ### Exercise 3 – Public Press Release

Developed a professional public news release highlighting Morgan State University's MSCHE reaffirmation for external audiences.

### Exercise 4 – Frequently Asked Questions

Created an executive FAQ explaining accreditation, reaffirmation, institutional impact, and future priorities in plain language.

## Executive Insight

Executive leaders rarely create entirely new documents. Instead, they transform one authoritative source into multiple communication products tailored to different audiences while maintaining consistency, credibility, and strategic alignment.
